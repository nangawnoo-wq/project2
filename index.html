Sharks

Sharks are ancient fish that have been on Earth for millions of years, even before the dinosaurs. Unlike most fish, sharks do not have bones—their skeleton is made of cartilage. They have an incredible sense of smell, and their teeth are constantly replaced throughout their lives.

Image source: Wikipedia

Cats

Cats are very popular pets, often active in the early morning and evening. They are excellent jumpers and highly flexible. Adult cats usually meow only to communicate with people, and their purring can help their bones and muscles heal faster.

Image source: diasolliwercapitaais on Pinterest

Penguin

Penguins are flightless birds that use their wings as flippers to swim underwater, making them excellent hunters. Their black and white countershading helps hide them from predators. Most penguin species live in colder regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

Image source: Animals Artistry on Pinterest

Racoon

Raccoons are very smart. Their distinctive black eye mask helps them see better at night. Their flexible front paws allow them to open jars, door latches, and easily find food, even in trash cans.

Image source: Virginia Lovell on Pinterest

Corn snake

A corn snake is a popular pet because it is non-venomous and easy to handle. These snakes live mostly in the southeastern United States. They kill prey by constriction and can live over 20 years in captivity.

Image source: vischer on Pinterest
